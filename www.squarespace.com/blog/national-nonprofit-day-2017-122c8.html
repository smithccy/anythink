National Nonprofit Day 2017

August 17, 2017 Alyssa Wolff

In honor of National Nonprofit Day, we’re pleased to share several Squarespace websites that are dedicated to giving back. All of these organizations strive to make the world a better place, and we’re proud that our platform helps bring their missions to life.  

Illustrated Impact

With their newsletter and website, Illustrated Impact engages their audience with a wide variety of current affairs. They strive to educate readers through poignant anecdotes and inspiring illustrations. Through their work, they hope to create a community filled with compassion.

MotherCoders

By way of their part-time technology training program, which includes on-site childcare, MotherCoders provides women with children the opportunity to build the skills, knowledge, and professional network they need to move into our economy's fastest-growing employment sector.

Animal Lighthouse Rescue

With an estimated 200,000 stray dogs living on the streets of Puerto Rico, Animal Lighthouse Rescue is dedicated to saving these animals by educating local communities, establishing humane treatment options, and finding the dogs loving homes.  

Beloved Atlanta

Beloved Atlanta is a two-year residential home and program in Atlanta for adult women who are survivors of  prostitution, trafficking, and addiction. Since launching in 2012, they have dedicated themselves to providing counseling, education, employment assistance, and support groups to the hundreds of women looking to escape the cycle of sexual exploitation.  

C4Q

To increase economic opportunity in the tech industry, C4Q provides people across all ethnicities, genders, and socioeconomic backgrounds with the opportunity to learn to code, gain jobs in tech, and create the companies of the future.

Library for All

By building a cloud-based library platform with e-books curated for different regions in the developing world, Library for All believes in providing every individual the chance to learn, dream, and lift themselves out of poverty.
